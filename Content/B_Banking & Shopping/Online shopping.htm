Safe online shopping

Online shopping has become a very convenient and time-saving way of purchasing products without leaving home. It's really appealing as you just need to find the product, add it to your basket, give your personal data, choose the mode of transport and pay for it with your card. A few clicks and your product is on your way to you. However, buying online may pose a risk if you don't obey some safety rules.

Safety tips for online shopping

  • Use online stores of well-known brands. If you don’t know the store, check opinions about it.
  • Check whether the store website looks professionally:

- It should have contact details like address and a telephone number.

- It should have a clear policy, including deliveries and returns (be sure to read the policy).

- Check the owner’s details (whether the store can be found in the Polish Database of Entrepreneurs).

- Check the web address bar (check for any misspellings in the name).

  • Use credit cards, and not debit cards, for online shopping. Credit cards are better protected against fraud.

This should make you suspicious

  • Website of the online store is of low quality (poor grammar and quality of photos, indistinct logo, no policy).
  • Suspiciously low prices of products.
  • Link to an online store found in social media, sent in an e-mail or sms.
  • Very wide product range (for example, the store offers not only clothes but also electrical devices).
  • Negative opinions about the store.
  • Only one payment method such as wire transfer (other payment methods are temporarily unavailable).
  • No security padlock symbol in the address bar or a padlock with a warning triangle or a red strike over it (the connection isn't safe).

Note: The presence of the security padlock symbol doesn't mean that the website is safe.

I did the shopping at a fraudulent online store – what to do

  1. Inform your bank about the transaction as soon as possible.
  2. If you made a credit card payment at a fraudulent website, block your credit card.
  3. Report the incident at CERT Website.
  4. Report a crime to the police.
  5. Warn others.
