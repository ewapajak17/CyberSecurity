Safe online dating

A romance scam is a cybercrime which may happen in social media and dating apps. A scammer adopts a fake online identity to attract a victim and develop an online relationship with them. Scammers pretend to be trustworthy and caring, and gradually gain the victim’s trust. They may propose marriage and make plans to meet in person. However, because of different excuses, the meeting never happens. Instead, they tell about an unexpected problem they face and ask for money.

Notice a fake social media or dating profile

  • An unnaturally attractive profile photo which can be a photoshopped image of someone else.
  • Minimal profile information, very few photos.
  • A low number of connections.
  • Distant location which is an excuse for not being able to meet in person.

Avoid romance scams

  • Be careful what you post in social media. The more information you share, the easier it's for a scammer to get to know you better, know your hobbies and habits.
  • Don’t rush into a relationship, especially when you haven't met the person, ask a lot of questions to get to know the person better.
  • Don’t ignore red flags (the person avoids meeting in person).
  • Be careful with sending your photos or sharing private information with the person you hardly know as they may use them against you or try to extort you.
  • Be cautious with clicking links or visiting websites sent to you by a person you know only through social media or a dating site.
  • Don’t send money to anyone with whom you communicated only online.
  • Don’t share your financial information, logins and passwords with a person you met online.
  • If you have doubts, ask your close friend or a relative you trust for advice.

Common types of romance scams:

  • Sugar daddy scam – a scammer pretends to be a wealthy person looking for a younger mate.
  • Catfishing scam – a scammer creates a fake profile and uses someone else’s identity and photos or generates them with Artificial Intelligence.
  • Inheritance scam – a scammer says that they need to get married to obtain an inheritance. But first they ask for money to pay for their travel.
  • Military scam – a scammer pretends to work in the military overseas which is an excuse for not meeting in person. They may ask for money for travel expenses.
  • Social media scam – a scammer contacts you through Facebook, Instagram or WhatsApp and shows interest in you to get information about you and gain your trust.

You're are a victim of a romance scam – what to do

  1. Contact your bank if you made a payment to someone who you met on the Internet and that person stopped contacting you.
  2. Change your password if you shared it with the person that you met online.
  3. Save all your conversations which you've had with that person. It'll be used as evidence. You may do screenshots. How to make a screenshot in Windows?
  4. Report a scam to the police.
  5. Report a scam on the dating platform or in social media that you used to warn others.
