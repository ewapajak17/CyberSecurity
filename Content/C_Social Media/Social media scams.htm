Social media scams

Scams you may face in social media:

  • Investment scam
  • Romance scam
  • Online shopping scam
  • Fake job offers
  • Fake donation requests
  • Fake loans

Check out the trap simulation in Polish social media:

  1. Go to Social media simulation.
  2. Fill in your name and gender.
  3. Click Log in (Zaloguj).
  4. Click “Show traps” (Pokaż pułapki).

Signals that your account may be hacked:

  • You can’t log into your account.
  • Your e-mail, password or name has been changed.
  • Your family or friends receive strange messages from you and you know you haven’t sent them.
  • Friend requests have been sent to people that you don’t know.
  • You receive a notification that someone logged into your account and you know it hasn't been you.
  • You receive e-mails confirming changes to your account which you haven’t made.

Someone hacked my Facebook account – what to do

If you can access your account

  1. If you still have access to your account, log into it from a trusted device and reset your password.
  2. Change a password to your e-mail account connected with Facebook.
  3. Check if your personal details and settings aren't changed.
  4. Enable Two-factor authentication.if you haven’t used it so far.
  5. If you have other accounts for which you use the same password as the hacked one, change them immediately.
  6. Go to FB Report Page and follow the instructions.

If you can’t access your account

  1. Go to FB Help Centre.
  2. From the Policies and reporting dropdown, choose Hacked and fake accounts.
  3. In the Hacked accounts, click Get started.
  4. Answer the questions and follow the instructions.
  5. Report the incident to the police.
