Safe mobile phones

These days it's hard to imagine living without a mobile phone. We use them to contact other people, search for information, watch films, find locations, and pay. To make use of their multiple features, we install different applications. It's worth remembering about some safety precautions:

  • Never leave your mobile phone unattended in public places.
  • Enable automatic screen locking and protect your device with a password.
  • Download applications only from trusted sources (always check for how long the app has been in use, how many people use it and who published it).
  • Install only those applications that you really need. Some apps may have some security gaps and breach privacy. If you don't use an app anymore, uninstall it.
  • Regularly update mobile applications (you may enable automatic updates).
  • Install a mobile phone tracker (CellTrack, Google Service – your mobile phone must be on when tracked).
  • Don't click links sent to you in an SMS, especially when the message informs you about delay in payment, problem with your bank account or credit card (in such a case, visit the bank’s or another supplier's website and call them directly).
  • Never trust the Caller ID. Scammers can spoof the number and it can look like the real one.

The most common PHONE SCAMS are Vishing, Smishing and Quishing.
